Special Conditions

Career Adjustment Disorder

Clinically significant distress (DSM-5 criteria) within 3 months of job change/promotion. Its symptoms are insomnia, paralyzing indecision, and tearfulness after work calls.

Work-Related Anxiety Disorder

Excessive worry about performance, presentations, or feedback. Its symptoms are panic attacks before meetings, and physical nausea from notifications.

Imposter Syndrome as Anxiety Manifestation

Persistent fear of exposure as a "fraud," often comorbid with GAD. Its symptoms are overworking to "prove worth" and avoiding leadership opportunities.

Digital Burnout (Occupational Phenomenon)

WHO-recognized syndrome from chronic workplace tech stress. Its symptoms are zoom fatigue, 24/7 hypervigilance, and emotional numbness.

Career Identity Crisis

Loss of self-concept when work conflicts with values (common in 20s-30s). Its symptoms areexistential dread, "golden handcuffs" guilt, and purposelessness.
