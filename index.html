Cognitive Behavioral Enhancement (CBE)

Transform How You Think, Feel, and Live

Cognitive Behavioral Enhancement (CBE) is a modern evolution of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), designed to identify, challenge, and reframe harmful thought patterns while building lifelong coping skills. Unlike generic approaches, CBE focuses on empowerment - equipping you with strategies to manage triggers, reduce anxiety, and prevent relapse. At Positivty, our licensed therapists tailor CBE techniques to your unique needs, blending online/in-person sessions with practical tools for real-world results.

  • 72% of college students using CBE report improved focus and reduced panic attacks
  • 68% of working professionals credit CBE with better work-life balance
  • 65% CBE is equally effective for older adults (55+), with 65% experiencing reduced loneliness

Types of CBE Therapy Tailored to Your Struggles

1. Mindfulness-Integrated CBE

Combines CBT with mindfulness practices to help you manage stress and stay grounded. Ideal for overthinkers, chronic worriers, or anyone craving calm in chaos.

2. Trauma-Focused CBE

Uses safe, phased techniques to reprocess trauma and reduce PTSD symptoms. Helps silence flashbacks, nightmares, and hypervigilance.

3. Strength-Based CBE

Amplifies your natural resilience to tackle burnout, self-doubt, or life transitions. Turn “I can’t” into “I will” with actionable, confidence-building strategies.

4. Exposure & Response Prevention (ERP) CBE

Gradually confront fears to break compulsive cycles (OCD, phobias, panic attacks). Learn to tolerate uncertainty without rituals or avoidance.

4. Tech-Enhanced CBE

Blends AI tools, mood journals, and live sessions for self-paced, data-driven care. Track progress via wearable sync (heart rate, sleep) and therapist feedback.

Your Journey to Mental Wellness

The Step-by-Step CBE Process

Being collaborative and goal-oriented, CBE process is designed to equip you with lifelong tools. Here’s a clear breakdown of how it unfolds, from assessment to lasting change:

Step 1

Initial Assessment & Goal Setting

Your therapist conducts a thorough evaluation of your mental health history, triggers, and symptoms.

Step 2

Identifying Negative Thought Patterns

Learn to recognize automatic thoughts (e.g., catastrophizing, self-blame) through thought records

Step 3

Cognitive Restructuring

Challenge irrational thoughts (e.g., “I’ll fail”) and replace them with balanced ones (“I can prepare and try”)

Step 4

Behavioral Activation

Gradually reintroduce rewarding activities (e.g., exercise, socializing) to combat withdrawal or avoidance

Step 5

Skill-Building & Practice

Master techniques like mindfulness, problem-solving, or exposure exercises through guided practice

Step 6

Progress Review & Adjustments

Biweekly check-ins to assess improvement (e.g., symptom logs, mood charts)

Step 7

Relapse Prevention Planning

Create a personalized “maintenance toolkit” (e.g., crisis scripts, self-assessment schedules)

Step 8

Graduation & Follow-Up

Final session to review achievements and schedule optional booster sessions
